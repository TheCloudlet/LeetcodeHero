* 1. Primitives
- AND (=&=) :: Extraction and Masking. Used to filter out specific bits, acting as a precise sieve within the data stream.
- OR (=|=) :: Fusion and Setting. Used to forcefully set specific bits to 1, injecting state into the system.
- XOR (=^=) :: Toggling and Cancellation. The most elegant operation in BitOps. In Haskell, this acts as an involution; logically, when two identical states collide, they annihilate each other and return to "Emptiness" (0).
- NOT (=~=) :: Inversion. Reversing all states—the fundamental primitive for deriving the Two's Complement.
- Shift (=<<=, =>>=) :: Spatial Translation. Within the ALU, it takes only a single clock cycle to execute the equivalent of multiplying or dividing by powers of 2.

* 2. Means of Combination & Abstraction
** 2.1 Single Bit Targeting
Surgically targeting a specific index within a 32-bit state machine.

*** Set a Bit
#+BEGIN_SRC cpp
n |= (1 << i);
#+END_SRC
*** Clear a Bit
#+BEGIN_SRC cpp
n &= ~(1 << i);
#+END_SRC
*** Toggle a Bit
#+BEGIN_SRC cpp
n ^= (1 << i)
#+END_SRC
*** Test a Bit
#+BEGIN_SRC
(n >> i) & 1
#+END_SRC

** 2.2 Field Extraction & Masking
Going beyond single bits, this deals with contiguous Bit Fields. In VHDL, this is known as Wire Slicing.
*** Get Right/Left Most Byte (=& 0xFF=)
Extracting specific 8-bit boundaries.
#+BEGIN_SRC cpp
uint8_t lsb_byte = n & 0xFF;         // Masking the lower 8 bits
uint8_t msb_byte = (n >> 24) & 0xFF; // Shift then mask
#+END_SRC

*** BEXTR (Bit Field Extract)
The generalized pattern for slicing any [start, start + length) range.
#+BEGIN_SRC cpp
// Traditional C++ implementation
uint32_t mask = (1U << length) - 1;
uint32_t field = (n >> start) & mask;
#+END_SRC

** 2.3 LSB/MSB Isolation & Properties
Leveraging the mathematical properties of Two's Complement to instantly isolate the boundary states of the system. This forms the foundation of advanced data structures like the Fenwick Tree.
*** [#A] Get Right Most 1 Bit (LSB Extraction)
#+BEGIN_SRC
n & (-n)
#+END_SRC
*** [#A] Remove the Right Most 1 Bit
#+BEGIN_SRC
n & (n - 1)
#+END_SRC

*** Check Power of 2
#+BEGIN_SRC
if (n & (n - 1) == 0) {
  std::printf("Is Power of 2");
}
#+END_SRC
